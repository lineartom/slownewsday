What is this?

This app filters out news stories you don't really need to read -- oftentimes, all of them! -- while still keeping you informed if something Earth-shattering has happened.

When it does show a story, it does so in plain, boring language with nothing to click. Take a glance and move on with your day!

FAQ

What if I'd rather see more news, or less news?
You can choose your preference from three different filter algorithms! Double-tap the page to cycle through them, or use the buttons below to save your preference. Quiet will quite often show nothing at all. Top-Four will always show exactly four stories, still in plain, boring language.
How often is it updated?
Once a day, at 5 AM Eastern. The point is to check it once and move on with your day. Do not bother refreshing again later.
Can I install this as an App?
Yes, but it's not in the App Store. It is designed to be installed as a Progressive Web App (PWA) on iOS and Android. Just open the menu in your browser and look for "Add to Home Screen" or similar.
Couldn't you link to a site carrying the full story?
Nope. The point is to reduce information overload and scrolling. The act of opening a separate app is one more filter for whether it's really important enough (to you) to read more.
How does it work?
The old gaming PC in my basement fetches public RSS feeds and then runs them through a small LLM that fits on the local GPU, an RTX 3060. I make a couple of passes over the data to improve results and make up for the small model. The resulting headlines are synthesized in the model - they are not copies of any one particular headline.
But I could have done this better!
I made this for myself, because (1) it was a thing I wanted and (2) I wanted to try out vibe coding. It is an explicit design goal to be simple and boring. If you want to make a better one, the best thing to do is probably invest in a larger LLM for the news analysis. Please do not add links, pictures, accounts, like-buttons, etc., I beg of you.
